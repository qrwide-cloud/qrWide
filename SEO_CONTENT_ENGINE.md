# QRWide SEO Content Engine

This document is a practical SEO operating system for `qrwide.com`.
It is designed for a Next.js App Router SaaS that wants to rank for QR-code-related searches, earn topical authority, and convert organic traffic into QR generation and signups.

Primary CTA across the site:
- `Generate QR Code Free`

Primary business goal:
- Win SEO traffic from high-intent QR code searches and move visitors into QR generation, account creation, and later plan upgrades.

Core product strengths to push in SEO:
- Free QR code generator
- Dynamic QR codes
- QR code tracking and analytics
- Bulk QR code generation
- WiFi QR codes
- vCard QR codes
- Restaurant menu QR codes
- Event QR codes
- Social QR codes
- PDF QR codes
- App download QR codes

## 1. Strategic Positioning

QRWide should not try to win only on the head term `qr code generator` first.
That term is crowded with mature competitors such as QR Code Monkey, Uniqode, QR TIGER, Beaconstac, Bitly, and Canva.

The fastest path is:

1. Win highly specific, lower-competition, high-conversion pages first.
2. Build dense topic clusters around each QR use case.
3. Use programmatic SEO for generators, use cases, and comparisons.
4. Use guides and blog content to support commercial pages.
5. Use comparison pages and “best” pages to siphon middle- and bottom-funnel demand.

Priority definitions used below:
- `P1`: easiest/fastest to rank, best early ROI
- `P2`: medium competition, should be built after P1 cluster foundations
- `P3`: high-competition head terms and comparison pages requiring stronger authority

| --- | --- | --- |

| `/tools/[tool-name]` | Utility/tool pages beyond core types | `/tools/bulk-qr-code-generator` |

| `/best/[topic]` | Commercial investigation pages | `/best/qr-code-generator-for-restaurants` |

### Recommended Hub Pages

| Hub | Why It Exists |
| --- | --- |
| `/qr-code-generator` | Main category hub for all QR generator variants |
| `/tools` | Hub for utilities like bulk, image-to-QR, logo QR, social QR |
| `/industries` | Hub for use-case pages by vertical |
| `/guides` | Hub for evergreen informational content |
| `/blog` | Fresh content, thought leadership, distribution |
| `/compare` | Competitor alternatives and comparison hub |
| `/best` | Commercial shortlist pages and “best X” SERPs |

### URL Strategy

- Use lowercase slugs only.
- Prefer exact-intent slugs over cute wording.
- Keep one canonical page per core intent.
- Avoid creating both `/qr-code-generator/pdf` and `/qr-code-generator-for-pdf` unless one 301s to the other.

Recommended primary canonical structure:
- tool/type intent: `/qr-code-generator/[type]`
- industry intent: `/industries/[industry]`
- comparison intent: `/compare/[competitor]`
- educational intent: `/guides/[topic]`

### 6.4 Next.js App Router Static Generation Strategy

For all predictable SEO pages:
- use `generateStaticParams()`
- use `generateMetadata()`
- pre-render pages at build time
- revalidate periodically for freshness

Recommended defaults:

```ts
export const dynamicParams = false
export const revalidate = 86400
```

Use:
- static generation for generators, tools, industries, comparison pages, best pages
- MDX-driven static generation for guides and blog
- no client-heavy hydration above the fold on SEO pages

Important App Router recommendations:
- keep page copy server-rendered
- lazy-load the interactive QR tool below the fold when possible
- use a small hero generator widget, but avoid shipping the full create-app payload to every SEO page

### 9.1 Topic Clusters to Own

2. Dynamic vs static QR codes

4. QR design and print best practices
5. QR use cases by industry

## 13. Page Speed and Core Web Vitals

For SEO pages specifically:
- keep the above-the-fold section mostly server-rendered
- defer heavy QR customizer bundles
- do not load the full dashboard app shell on marketing pages
- use static generation for content pages
- optimize fonts and avoid render-blocking design flourishes
- lazy-load charts, big screenshots, and non-critical testimonials

Critical CWV targets:
- LCP under 2.5s
- INP under 200ms
- CLS under 0.1

Specific Next.js recommendations:
- use `generateMetadata()` on server
- use `next/image` for all content images
- split the “interactive generator” from the “SEO content page”
- keep metadata, FAQ schema, and body copy available without client JS
